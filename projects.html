Computational Cancer Biology

This project focuses on understanding cancer biology using computational approaches including bioinformatics, gene expression analysis, and multi-omics data integration.

Cytogenetics and Chromosomal Instability

Research in this area focuses on identifying chromosomal abnormalities associated with cancer using cytogenetic techniques and computational analysis.

AI-Driven Discovery of Cryptic Regulatory Interfaces in KRAS Signaling

This project investigates hidden regulatory interfaces in KRAS signaling pathways using molecular dynamics simulations, machine learning, and structural bioinformatics. The goal is to identify novel therapeutic targets in oncogenic KRAS signaling through computational discovery of cryptic pockets and regulatory surfaces.

Natural GLP-1R Modulator Discovery (N-GLP1M)

This project focuses on identifying natural compounds capable of modulating the GLP-1 receptor using computational drug discovery approaches including molecular docking, virtual screening, and molecular dynamics simulations. The goal is to discover novel natural modulators with potential applications in metabolic disorders such as diabetes and obesity.

Environmental–Epigenomic Network Rewiring as a Determinant of Cancer Susceptibility

This project explores how environmental exposures influence epigenomic networks that regulate cancer susceptibility. By integrating multi-omics datasets and computational network analysis, the project aims to identify epigenetic mechanisms linking environmental stressors to cancer development.

Endocrine Disrupter and Cancer Risk in Rural India: Cytogenetic and Epigenetic Insights

This project investigates the relationship between endocrine-disrupting environmental chemicals and cancer risk in rural populations. The research integrates cytogenetic analysis, epigenetic profiling, and computational data analysis to understand how environmental exposures influence genomic stability and cancer susceptibility.
